{% extends "layout.html" %} {% block heading %} How to use calculator {% endblock %} {% block body %} Term:
Represent (in the number of years) the length of the remaining expected life of the option from the date of grant.

Strike price:
This is the strike/exercise price indicated in the option agreement.

Stock price:
This is the closing price of the underlying equity at the date of grant

Risk Free Rate:
This is the risk free rate based on public money market rates or other government debt rate. Please make sure that the rate selected matches the term (See 'Term' above) of the option being valued.

Number of options:
This is the number of options issued that are being valued.

Dividend Yield Rate:
If the underlying equity doesn't pay dividends, then put in 0.00 in the calculator. If the underlying equity does indeed pay dividends, then please add enter that in the calculator.

Ticker:
This is the symbol of the underlying equity being valued.

Grant date:
This is the date on which the options were issued to the option holder.

Look back date:
Since this calculator uses historical volatility as standard deviation of logarithmic returns, based on daily closing prices, it requires a look back date.
The look back date is essentially the grant date minus the Term of the option.
For example, for an option that was issued on June 1st 2020 and has a term of 5 years, then the
approximate look back date would be June 1st 2015.
